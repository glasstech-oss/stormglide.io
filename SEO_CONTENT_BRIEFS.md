## **GUIDE #5: How to Choose Between Custom and Off-the-Shelf Software**

### **Target Keywords**
- Custom vs off-the-shelf software (Primary)
- When to build vs buy software
- Custom software vs SaaS comparison
- Should I build or buy software

### **Purpose**
High-intent decision-stage content. Businesses evaluating whether to build custom, buy existing, or hybrid approach. Position StormGlide as the advisor (not just vendor).

### **Word Count Target**: 2000-2500 words

### **Section Outline**

1. **Introduction** (150-200 words)
   - Common mistake: Choosing wrong approach (wastes time/money)
   - This guide: Decision framework, comparison, real examples
   - Context: StormGlide does both (custom development + own SaaS products)
   - Reader outcome: Know which path is right for your business

2. **Part 1: Off-the-Shelf Software (SaaS)** (300-400 words)
   - Definition: Pre-built software, subscription model
   - Examples: Zoho, Monday.com, Asana, NetSuite
   - Advantages:
     * Fast deployment (weeks, not months)
     * Lower cost ($50-$500/month typically)
     * Regular updates and support
     * Security and compliance handled by vendor
   - Disadvantages:
     * Limited to vendor's feature set
     * Customization constraints
     * Data ownership concerns
     * Vendor lock-in (switching costly)
   - Best for: Standard processes, quick time-to-value, budget-conscious

3. **Part 2: Custom Software** (300-400 words)
   - Definition: Built specifically for your business
   - Can be owned (you own code), or outsourced (vendor owns, you pay for dev)
   - Advantages:
     * Tailored to exact business needs
     * Competitive advantage (custom workflows)
     * Full data ownership
     * Long-term cost effectiveness
     * Scalability as you grow
   - Disadvantages:
     * Higher upfront cost ($30K-$500K+)
     * Longer timeline (3-12 months)
     * Ongoing maintenance responsibility
     * Requires vendor relationship or internal team
   - Best for: Complex processes, competitive advantage, long-term operations

4. **Part 3: Hybrid Approach** (250-350 words)
   - Use off-the-shelf for standard functions (accounting, HR, CRM)
   - Build custom for competitive advantage (unique logistics, specialized workflows)
   - Integration layer connects them
   - **StormGlide example**: Use accounting SaaS + custom inventory system
   - Often the sweet spot: Cost-effective + differentiation

5. **Part 4: Decision Framework** (400-500 words)
   - **Question 1**: Is this core to your competitive advantage?
     * YES → Custom
     * NO → Off-the-shelf
   - **Question 2**: How unique is your business process?
     * Very unique (90%+ different from industry standard) → Custom
     * Standard (aligned with industry norms) → Off-the-shelf
   - **Question 3**: What's your timeline?
     * Need it in 4 weeks → Off-the-shelf
     * Can wait 6-12 weeks → Custom
   - **Question 4**: What's your budget?
     * <$50K → Off-the-shelf
     * $50K-$500K → Custom or hybrid
     * >$500K → Enterprise custom or multiple SaaS platforms
   - **Question 5**: How much customization do you need?
     * 0-20% custom → Off-the-shelf
     * 20-60% custom → Hybrid
     * 60%+ custom → Custom
   - **Question 6**: Will this change frequently?
     * Yes, very dynamic → Custom (more flexible)
     * No, stable → Off-the-shelf (vendor manages updates)
   - Decision matrix: Create visual showing optimal choice

6. **Part 5: Cost & Timeline Reality Check** (300-400 words)
   - **Off-the-shelf costs**:
     * Implementation: $5K-$50K
     * Monthly: $100-$1000+
     * Setup time: 2-8 weeks
     * Total first-year cost: $20K-$150K
   - **Custom software costs**:
     * Development: $50K-$500K
     * Setup time: 8-24 weeks
     * Ongoing: $10K-$50K/year (maintenance)
     * Total first-year cost: $60K-$550K
   - **Break-even**: When custom pays for itself vs repeated SaaS fees
     * Example: $100/month SaaS = $1200/year × 5 years = $6K vs $50K custom (custom loses)
     * Example: $1000/month SaaS = $12K/year × 5 years = $60K vs $50K custom (custom wins)

7. **Part 6: Real Business Examples** (300-400 words)
   - **Case 1: Lollarod** → Chose hybrid (off-the-shelf e-commerce + custom admin backoffice)
     * Why: Standard e-commerce exists, but needed custom inventory + multi-location management
     * Outcome: Faster time-to-market, competitive advantage
   
   - **Case 2: Logistics Company** → Built custom CargoScan instead of buying Samsara/Verizon
     * Why: Needed offline capability, local payment integration (not available in off-the-shelf)
     * Outcome: Better for African market, recurring revenue model
   
   - **Case 3: Manufacturing Company** → Chose custom ERP (Nexus MFG) vs SAP
     * Why: SAP too expensive ($200K+), too complex; needed simpler, affordable solution
     * Outcome: Operational efficiency, better ROI

8. **Part 7: Hidden Costs to Consider** (200-300 words)
   - Off-the-shelf hidden costs:
     * Time to customize (even if limited)
     * Training staff
     * Migration from old system
     * Vendor price increases
     * Switching costs if you outgrow it
   - Custom hidden costs:
     * Change requests during development
     * Extended timelines
     * Technical debt accumulation
     * Security updates and maintenance

9. **Conclusion + CTA** (150-200 words)
   - Summary: Choose custom for competitive advantage, off-the-shelf for speed/cost
   - Most businesses: Hybrid approach works best
   - CTA: "Not sure which is right for your business? Let's discuss"
   - Link to: /services (custom development), /nexus-hrm (SaaS product), /contact
